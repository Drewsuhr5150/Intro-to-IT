Project Idea

I’d like to create a social hub app for gamers to meet other gamers in their area so that they can team up in their favourite games on their chosen platform at any time of the day/night and develop personal relationships online and possibly in person. While there are similar products out there, I struggled to find one that streamlines this process as they are cluttered with social news feeds like Facebook and Twitter or are locked behind and a pay wall or other requirements. The aim would be to have a profile that can help you instantly connect you with someone who shares your interests and style of gaming.

My personal interest in such a product is the main motivation for the project. I have a large circle of friends who I’ve met through Australia’s music scene, people I’ve performed with, worked with, shared a drink at the local music bar or concert hall while watching other bands as well as social media platforms that promote musicians and this has me constantly meeting new people who I share interests with and making new friends. Unfortunately, none of these friends are all that interested in gaming so over the years I’ve found myself playing a lot of multiplayer games by myself not getting the full experience as I’ve not managed to make a connection with other gamers. As games are usually played at home in your own space it can in some ways promote anti-social behaviour so the aim of the app would be to connect people and enjoy the social side of the gaming as I have experienced similarly through music.

This would be a free social networking app on mobile devices for gamers to quickly make friends/connections with other gamers online. The service would be available to people aged 16 and over.

Users would download the app from their chosen marketplace (android, IOS, windows) and sign up to the service with an email. The app would include a basic profile creation tool where you can add as much information as you choose to disclose like a profile picture, name, gender, age, general location, time active and more pictures. Your profile will also include your preferred platform/platforms and you can choose which specific games or genre you like to play as well as your play style (beginner, advanced, casual, party, story, competitive).

There will be an additional contact window where users can provide their username/gamertag to other services like PlayStation network, Xbox live, Steam, Discord, YouTube etc. From here the app will display other users’ profiles in your country where you can choose to tap on their profile and seek more information or simply decline the profile and see the next one. If the user finds common ground with another profile they can send a friend request to this person, they will be notified through the app and choose whether they’d like to communicate with the requestee or not.

Once the user has befriended another profile, they’ll appear in a friends list where they can communicate through text, audio, images and possibly video. The user can remove a friend at any time for whatever reason, possibly inactivity of the other gamer, content unrelated to gaming or inappropriate communications making the user feel uncomfortable/unsafe. If the user has had a positive experience with another profile, they can commend this person with compliments that work as in-app badges that will display on their profile.

Badges may include friendly manner, a good sport, a good shot, right hand man (co-op partner), team player, team leader, achievement hunter, completionist.

The app will have a search engine where the user search for a particular profile or can do a more specific search for other profiles limiting the location to a city/town and distance between the user and others, particular gender, age group, favourite games, favourite genre, play style and their preferred time of day to play.

Additional features of the app will the ability to create squad/guild where multiple friends can communicate and see when one another are online, the option to link your game account from PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo, Steam, Mobile gaming profile displaying your games completed and achievements.

Further development could result creating online events/tournaments through the service to further enrich the social aspect perhaps even in person events where people can meet to play and compete in the same room.

An app development program would be required to undertake the project as well as function testing tools like Appcelerator, Titanium, PhoneGap, jQuery Mobile, appery.io:, iBuild App: suggested at -www.bbvaapimarket.com These can easily be found online and require a monthly or annual subscription to use. I would choose one of these programs based on price and functionality.
